Project 3: Non-linear Narrative

For this project, we had to create a non-linear narrative using Twine, while using css, html, and the Twine cookbook.

The most effective part of this project were the great feedback I received from my group members and how special this Twine narrative was to me as well. Some of the feedback I received from my group members:

- "I really loved how Spanish wass incorporated into the story. It really made it feel like a real time story and real experiences" - Lexi

- "Hearing the inner monologue of the characters made them very relatable..." - Jon Barker

- "There was a build up in the narrative arc that kept you guessing literally until the end" - John Barker

- "The character dialogue and narration kept me immersed in the story..." - Ada Wang

Personally, I got inspired to make this narrative because of the chapters we had to read from Anna Anthropy. The author constantly emphasized how if videogames were created by a more diverse team, different stories and perspectives could be shown to the world. As a result, I thought of creating a Twine game that gave perspective to the racism, inequality, and all of the emotions that come with the immigration process.
